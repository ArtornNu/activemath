Wormhole Transverse · a companion game for Module 5

Enter the Construct

Every point in this space can be found — and returned to — through a Matrix. Four ideas hold the whole Construct together:

The Prime Construct — the intact dimension. Its reference frame is the Identity Matrix, anchored at (0, 0), traced by the Standard Basis.
The Warp — the distortion. A disturbance transforms that Matrix, bending it away from Identity.
The Multiverse — the fallout. The distortion didn't erase anything; it split off entangled sub-dimensions, each one a Verse of its own.
The Bridge — the way back. Solving the math — finding the exact ratio (m, n) a point sits at — IS building the bridge back to the Prime Construct.

Welcome, Operator. Across four Trials you'll decode a Jack Point, reverse the signal, then measure how much the Warp bends space itself. No formulas are named up front — you'll meet each one by doing the thing it describes first.

For facilitatorsThis is a standalone companion for Module 5's matrix activities — best played after Activities 2, 3, 5 and 6, since each Trial quietly reuses one of those ideas (matching a shape, change of basis, area/determinant, inverse) without naming it up front. Works equally well as a warm-up recap or a closing consolidation game.

Debrief

What you just did, named

Trial I was matrix–vector multiplication: x = a·m + b·n, y = c·m + d·n.

Trial II was the inverse matrix M⁻¹ — undoing that same multiplication.

Trial III was the determinant, ad − bc — the factor by which the Warp stretches or shrinks area, and the exact number that decides whether Trial II even has an answer.

For facilitatorsConsider replaying Trial III as a group and deliberately hunting for a Glitch (det ≈ 0) and a Mirror Anomaly (det < 0) together — both are easy to trigger and make excellent whole-room discussion prompts about what a "structure" can and can't do.
