Mathematics for Equity and Social Justice

Using data collection and mathematical modeling so students investigate real questions of fairness in their own communities — math as a tool for voice, not just computation.

Planned outline

  • Framing quote: Paulo Freire — "the oppressed must be actively engaged at every step of gaining their own freedom" — as the philosophical anchor for the session.
  • Case study — the zebra crossing project: a Grade 6 student (referred to as "Kota") counts how many cars fail to stop for pedestrians at a crossing near his school, turns the tally into a data report, and sends it to the local police — who use his statistics to design a road-safety campaign flyer. A real example of data + advocacy.
  • Hands-on activity — the ring-toss game: younger and older students play together and must renegotiate the rules (throwing distance, number of turns) so the game stays fair for both groups — a concrete entry point into probability, fairness, and equity.
  • Bridging to "for whom, by whom": whose interests does a "neutral" mathematical rule actually serve?
  • Facilitator guide: how to run sensitive equity conversations productively in a teacher-training room.
